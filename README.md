# Statistical Significance Translator

Enter a p-value. Get plain English, a stakeholder-ready sentence, and a list of what your result does and doesn't mean.

**[Try it live →](https://dekic648.github.io/significance-translator/)**

## What it does

Statistical significance is one of the most misunderstood concepts in research. This tool translates your test results into clear, accurate language suitable for stakeholders, reports, and slides.

Given your inputs (p-value, sample size, effect size, context), it provides:
- **Plain-English verdict** — what the result actually means
- **Effect size interpretation** with visual meter
- **Common misconceptions** specific to your result — what NOT to conclude
- **A copy-paste stakeholder sentence** calibrated to your context

## Why this exists

Researchers frequently over-claim ("p < 0.05 proves our design is better") or under-claim ("not significant means no difference"). Both are wrong. This tool enforces accurate interpretation every time.

## Inputs

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| p-value | Your test's p-value |
| Sample size | Total n |
| Effect size | Small / medium / large (Cohen's d or r) |
| What was tested | Task completion, time, satisfaction, errors, or general |
| Alpha threshold | 0.10, 0.05, or 0.01 |
| Publishing context | Internal (more lenient) vs. published research |
